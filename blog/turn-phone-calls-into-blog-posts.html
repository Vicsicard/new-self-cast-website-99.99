5 Steps to Turn Phone Calls Into Blog Posts

Step 1: Record the Phone Call

The first step is capturing the conversation. You have several options:

Recording Tools:

  • Rev Call Recorder (iOS): Free app for recording phone calls
  • TapeACall: Works on both iOS and Android
  • Zoom/Google Meet: Built-in recording for video calls
  • Otter.ai: Records and transcribes simultaneously
  • Professional services: Self Cast Studios handles recording for you

Important Legal Note: Always get consent before recording. In most states, you need permission from all parties. Start calls with: "I'm recording this conversation for content purposes. Is that okay with you?"

Pro Tips:

  • Use a quiet environment to minimize background noise
  • Test your recording setup before important calls
  • Use headphones for better audio quality
  • Have a backup recording method just in case

Step 2: Transcribe the Conversation

Once you have the recording, you need to convert audio to text. Here are your options:

Transcription Services:

  • Otter.ai: $8.33/month for 1,200 minutes (AI transcription, 95% accuracy)
  • Rev: $1.50/minute for human transcription (99% accuracy)
  • Descript: $12/month with editing tools included
  • Google Docs Voice Typing: Free but requires manual playback
  • Self Cast Studios: Transcription included in $99/month service

What to Expect:

  • AI transcription: Fast (minutes) but may have errors with technical terms
  • Human transcription: Slower (hours to days) but highly accurate
  • Hybrid approach: AI first, then human editing for best balance

Time Investment: If doing it yourself, budget 1-2 hours for a 30-minute call (playback, transcription, initial cleanup).

Step 3: Extract Key Points and Structure

Raw transcripts are messy—full of "ums," tangents, and conversational filler. Your job is to extract the gold.

How to Extract Key Points:

  1. Read through the transcript: Highlight main topics, interesting quotes, and actionable insights
  2. Identify themes: Group related points together
  3. Find the narrative: What's the main story or message?
  4. Pull best quotes: Mark authentic, quotable moments
  5. Note examples: Real-world stories make content engaging

Create an Outline:

  • Introduction: Hook + problem + what reader will learn
  • Main sections: 3-5 key points from the conversation
  • Supporting details: Examples, quotes, explanations
  • Conclusion: Recap + call to action

Pro Tip: Use AI tools like ChatGPT to help summarize transcripts and suggest outlines. Just paste the transcript and ask: "Extract the main points and create a blog post outline."

Step 4: Write and Format the Blog Post

Now transform your outline and key points into a polished blog post.

Writing Process:

  1. Start with the outline: Use your structure from Step 3
  2. Write conversationally: Maintain the authentic tone from the call
  3. Use direct quotes: Include the best verbatim moments
  4. Add transitions: Connect ideas smoothly
  5. Remove filler: Cut "ums," repetition, and tangents
  6. Add context: Explain references that need clarification

Formatting Best Practices:

  • Headers (H2, H3): Break content into scannable sections
  • Short paragraphs: 2-4 sentences max for readability
  • Bullet points: Use lists for easy scanning
  • Bold key phrases: Highlight important takeaways
  • Add images: Break up text with relevant visuals

Time Investment: Expect 2-3 hours to write and format a 1,500-word post from a transcript.

Step 5: Edit and Optimize for SEO

The final step is polishing your content and optimizing it for search engines.

Editing Checklist:

  • Grammar and spelling: Use Grammarly or similar tools
  • Flow and clarity: Read aloud to catch awkward phrasing
  • Fact-check: Verify statistics and claims mentioned
  • Tone consistency: Ensure voice matches your brand
  • Remove redundancy: Cut repeated points

SEO Optimization:

  • Title tag: Include primary keyword (e.g., "turn phone calls into blog posts")
  • Meta description: 155-160 characters with keyword and benefit
  • Headers: Use H2/H3 tags with keyword variations
  • Internal links: Link to related content on your site
  • External links: Link to 2-3 authoritative sources
  • Alt text: Add descriptive alt text to images
  • URL slug: Keep it short and keyword-rich

Final Review: Before publishing, ask yourself:

  • Does this provide value to readers?
  • Is the main point clear?
  • Would I want to read this?
  • Is it better than competing content on this topic?
