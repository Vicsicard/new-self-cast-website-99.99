What to Outsource (And What to Keep In-House)

Not everything should be outsourced. Here's the strategic breakdown:

Outsource These:

  • Blog post writing: Time-consuming, requires skill
  • Social media content: Consistent posting is tedious
  • SEO optimization: Technical expertise required
  • Content editing: Fresh eyes catch errors
  • Graphic design: Professional visuals matter
  • Email newsletters: Regular writing and formatting

Keep In-House:

  • Strategy and direction: You know your business best
  • Brand voice guidelines: Define how you want to sound
  • Subject matter expertise: Share your knowledge (via interviews)
  • Final approval: Review before publishing
  • Customer engagement: Respond to comments personally

How to Choose the Right Content Marketing Partner

Use this checklist to evaluate potential partners:

1. Budget Alignment

Questions to ask:

  • What's the total monthly cost?
  • Are there setup fees or minimums?
  • What's included in the base price?
  • What costs extra (revisions, rush jobs, etc.)?

2. Quality Standards

Request:

  • Writing samples in your industry
  • Client testimonials or case studies
  • Information about their writers/editors
  • Revision policy

3. Process and Communication

Understand:

  • How do you provide input/direction?
  • What's the turnaround time?
  • How many revisions are included?
  • Who's your point of contact?
  • How do you receive final content?

4. Scalability

Consider:

  • Can you increase/decrease volume easily?
  • Are there penalties for pausing?
  • Can they handle rush projects?
  • What's the maximum output capacity?

5. Ownership and Rights

Clarify:

  • Do you own the content 100%?
  • Can you use it anywhere?
  • Are there any licensing restrictions?
  • What happens if you cancel?

Common Mistakes When Outsourcing Content Marketing

Mistake 1: Choosing Based on Price Alone

The cheapest option often delivers the worst results. Focus on value: quality content that drives results is worth paying for.

Mistake 2: Not Providing Enough Direction

Even the best writers need guidance. Share your brand voice, target audience, and goals clearly.

Mistake 3: Expecting Instant Results

Content marketing is a long game. Give it 3-6 months to see meaningful SEO and traffic results.

Mistake 4: Not Reviewing Content Before Publishing

Always review and approve content. You're responsible for what goes out under your brand.

Mistake 5: Ignoring Analytics

Track what's working. Use Google Analytics to see which content drives traffic and conversions.
