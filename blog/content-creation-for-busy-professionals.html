Strategy 2: Repurpose Existing Work

Time Investment: 30-60 minutes per month
Output: 2-3 blog posts

You're already creating valuable content—client presentations, webinars, speaking engagements, internal memos. Repurpose them.

What to repurpose:

  • Client presentations: Turn slides into blog posts (remove confidential info)
  • Webinars/podcasts: Transcribe and edit into articles
  • Speaking notes: Expand into thought leadership pieces
  • Email responses: Common client questions make great FAQs
  • Internal training: Adapt for public consumption

Pro tip: Hire a VA or content assistant to handle the repurposing. You provide raw material, they create the content.

Strategy 3: Batch Content Creation

Time Investment: 2-3 hours quarterly
Output: 3 months of content

Block one afternoon per quarter for content creation. Record 3-4 voice memos or video recordings covering different topics. Send to a service for transcription and editing.

Batching benefits:

  • Easier to get into "content mode" once vs. weekly
  • More efficient than scattered efforts
  • Ensures consistent publishing schedule
  • Reduces decision fatigue

Strategy 4: Curate + Comment

Time Investment: 15 minutes per week
Output: 4 LinkedIn posts per month

Share industry articles with your expert commentary. This positions you as a thought leader without creating original content from scratch.

Formula:

  1. Find relevant industry article
  2. Write 2-3 sentences on why it matters
  3. Add your unique perspective or experience
  4. Post to LinkedIn with link

Takes 5 minutes per post, builds authority, keeps you visible.

Strategy 5: Micro-Content Approach

Time Investment: 10 minutes per day
Output: Daily social media presence

Not every piece of content needs to be a 2,000-word blog post. Micro-content keeps you visible with minimal time investment.

Micro-content ideas:

  • Quick tips: 1-2 sentence insights
  • Stats + commentary: Share relevant data with your take
  • Behind-the-scenes: Quick photo/update from your work
  • Questions: Engage your network with thought-provoking questions
  • Wins: Share client successes (with permission)

Content Types That Work for Busy Professionals

Not all content is created equal. Focus on high-ROI formats:

1. Thought Leadership Articles

Best for: Establishing expertise and attracting high-value clients
Frequency: 2-4 per month
Length: 800-1,500 words

Share insights on industry trends, challenges, and solutions. Position yourself as the expert clients want to hire.

2. Case Studies (Anonymized)

Best for: Demonstrating results and methodology
Frequency: 1 per quarter
Length: 1,000-1,500 words

Showcase your work without violating confidentiality. Focus on the problem, approach, and results.

3. LinkedIn Posts

Best for: Staying top-of-mind with your network
Frequency: 2-3 per week
Length: 100-300 words

Short, valuable insights that keep you visible without major time investment.

4. Updated Bio/About Page

Best for: First impressions and credibility
Frequency: Quarterly updates
Length: 300-500 words

Keep your professional bio current with recent accomplishments and expertise.
